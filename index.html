Zaha Hadid

A Tribute to the Queen of Curves

Timeline of Zaha Hadid’s Life and Achievements

  • 1950 – Born in Baghdad, Iraq.
  • 1980 – Moved to London to study architecture at the Architectural Association School.
  • 1983 – Gained international recognition with her competition-winning design for The Peak in Hong Kong (though it was never built).
  • 2004 – Became the first woman ever to receive the Pritzker Architecture Prize, often considered the Nobel of architecture.
  • 2010 – Completed the MAXXI – National Museum of the 21st Century Arts in Rome, winning critical acclaim.
  • 2012 – Honored with the title Dame by Queen Elizabeth II.
  • 2016 – Passed away unexpectedly in Miami at the age of 65, leaving a legacy of daring design and structural elegance.

Conclusion

Zaha Hadid was not just an architect; she was a symbol of innovation, courage, and breaking barriers. She left an indelible mark on the world of architecture, paving the way for women in a traditionally male-dominated field. Her legacy of revolutionary buildings and futuristic designs will continue to inspire generations to come, proving that creativity knows no limits.
